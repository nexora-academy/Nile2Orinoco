Uber and Careem are the safest options, providing fixed fares and GPS tracking.

Use the women-only carriages in the Metro and avoid walking alone in isolated areas late at night.

Modesty is appreciated. Cover shoulders and knees when visiting mosques or traditional local markets.

A polite "La Shukran" (No thank you) and a smile is usually enough. Keep walking confidently.

Small tips are standard for services (waiters, porters). 10-20 EGP is a common small gesture.

Cards are accepted in hotels and malls, but always carry Egyptian Pounds for bazaars and local shops.

Photography with phones is free in most sites. Avoid photographing military or police buildings.

Remove shoes before entering. Women should cover their hair with a scarf (often provided at entrance).

The sun is strong. Always wear sunscreen, a hat, and stay hydrated with bottled water.

Drones are strictly prohibited without a military permit. Do not bring them to the airport.

Available at the airport from providers like Vodafone or Orange. Essential for maps and safety.

Wait for a local and walk alongside them. Egyptian drivers expect steady movement.

Alcohol is served in licensed hotels/bars. Public consumption of alcohol is not permitted.

Early morning (8 AM) is best to avoid heat and the largest crowds.

Wash hands frequently and use hand sanitizer. Stick to hot, well-cooked meals.

Private hospitals in major cities offer excellent care and most doctors speak English.

Bargaining is a tradition. Start at 50% of the price and meet in the middle.

Keep your original passport in the hotel safe and carry a clear photocopy with you at all times.
