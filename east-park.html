East Park: Nature's Canvas

Explore the lush landscapes of Caracas' favorite escape, where tranquil lagoons and tropical wildlife create a living masterpiece.

Designed as a tropical haven, Parque del Este (Parque Generalísimo Francisco de Miranda) is a vibrant ecosystem in the heart of the city. From the iconic Leander ship to the paddle boats on the lake, the park offers a serene retreat. Observe exotic species like the Orinoco crocodile and colorful macaws while walking through curated gardens that reflect Venezuela's rich biodiversity.

Caracas' Green Sanctuary

East Park: Nature’s Masterpiece in the City

Escape the urban concrete and breathe within 200 acres of pure, tropical serenity designed by the legendary Roberto Burle Marx.

Beyond the bustling streets of Caracas lies a world where the Leander Ship rests in quiet lagoons and pink flamingos grace the water's edge. East Park isn't just a park; it’s a living museum of Venezuelan biodiversity, offering a perfect horizon where the lush botanical trails meet the majestic silhouette of Mount Avila.
