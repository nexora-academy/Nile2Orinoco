The Religious Complex

Experience a unique spiritual journey in the heart of Old Cairo, where the world's three monotheistic religions embrace in one square kilometer.

Known as the "Coptic Cairo," this area houses the Hanging Church, the Ben Ezra Synagogue, and the Mosque of Amr ibn al-Aas. It stands as a living testament to Egypt's long-standing tradition of religious tolerance and cultural diversity, spanning over two millennia of history.

Religious Complex: A Cradle of Faith

Explore the unique square kilometer in Old Cairo where the world's three monotheistic religions have coexisted for centuries.

The Religious Complex in Fustat is a global symbol of peaceful coexistence. Within its ancient boundaries, you can visit the Mosque of Amr ibn al-Aas (the first mosque in Africa), several of the oldest Coptic churches like the Hanging Church, and the Ben Ezra Synagogue. This sacred site tells the story of Egypt as a melting pot of civilizations, preserving thousands of years of spiritual architecture and holy traditions.
