Angel Falls: Nature's Eternal Skyscraper

Discover the awe-inspiring beauty of Kerepakupai Merú. Dropping from the massive Auyán-tepui, this is not just a waterfall; it's a testament to the raw power of the Venezuelan jungle.

Known as the world's highest uninterrupted waterfall, Angel Falls plunges from a height of 979 meters. The water turns into a mystical mist before even reaching the ground, creating a prehistoric atmosphere that feels like stepping back into a lost world.

Angel Falls: A Geological Wonder

Discover the untamed beauty of the Auyán-tepui, where the world's highest waterfall carves its path through ancient stone.

Towering over the Venezuelan jungle, Angel Falls is a breathtaking display of nature's vertical grandeur. Named after Jimmy Angel, the aviator who revealed it to the world, this cascade falls from a "Tepui" (Table-top mountain) that is billions of years old. The sheer scale and the mystical mist that rises from the abyss make it one of the most spiritually and visually captivating places on our planet.
