Saint Catherine Monastery

Step into one of the oldest working Christian monasteries in the world, a fortress of faith nestled at the foot of Mount Sinai.

Founded in the 6th century by Emperor Justinian, the monastery protects the site of the "Burning Bush" and houses a library of ancient manuscripts second only to the Vatican. It is a place where history, spirituality, and the rugged beauty of the Sinai desert meet.

St. Catherine: A Spiritual Fortress

Discover the world's oldest continuously inhabited Christian monastery, a sanctuary of peace at the base of Mount Sinai.

Built between 548 and 565 AD, the Monastery of Saint Catherine is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a living symbol of harmony. It protects the sacred "Burning Bush" where Moses is said to have encountered the divine. Beyond its massive granite walls, it houses a staggering collection of rare icons and ancient manuscripts, standing as a timeless guardian of spiritual wisdom and religious history.
