The Great Library of Alexandria

A rebirth of the ancient world's most famous center of learning. Today's Bibliotheca Alexandrina is a masterpiece of modern architecture and a global crossroads for culture.

Standing on the shores where the original library once flourished, this sun-disk shaped marvel houses millions of books across eleven cascading levels. Beyond its massive reading halls, it hosts museums of antiquities and manuscripts, a planetarium, and an intricate wall engraved with characters from 120 different world scripts.

Bibliotheca: The Temple of Wisdom

Rising like a second sun over the Mediterranean, the Library of Alexandria stands as a testament to humanity's eternal quest for knowledge.

Inaugurated in 2002, this modern masterpiece is more than just a archive of books; it is a complex of museums, galleries, and a planetarium. The main reading hall can accommodate 2,000 readers under its stunning tilted roof, while the granite facade is carved with characters from almost every known alphabet, bridging the gap between ancient civilizations and the digital age.
